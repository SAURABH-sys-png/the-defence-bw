The National Defence Academy (NDA) written exam remains one of the most competitive entry points into the armed forces. Clearing it isn't about solving every question; it's about tactical selection.

## The Mathematics Trap

Many aspirants fail not because they don't know math, but because they mismanage time. The math paper contains 120 questions to be solved in 150 minutes. That is roughly 75 seconds per question.

*   **Phase 1 (First 40 Mins):** Target high-yield, low-time topics like Matrices, Determinants, Probability, and Statistics.
*   **Phase 2 (Next 60 Mins):** Dive into Calculus and Trigonometry only if your foundational concepts are rock-solid. 

> **Rule of Thumb:** If a geometry or calculus problem takes more than 4 steps to crack during practice, skip it on the first pass during the actual exam.

## General Ability Test (GAT) Allocation

The GAT paper is where you balance your score sheet. Focus heavily on English idioms, synonyms, and basic physics/chemistry metrics rather than getting bogged down by expansive history timelines.
