## Introduction to CDS

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) examination is the gateway to becoming an officer in the Indian Armed Forces. It's a highly competitive examination that requires strategic preparation and clarity about the selection process.

## Eligibility Criteria

### Age Limits (As of CDS 2026)
- **Officers' Training Academy (OTA)**: 19-25 years (women candidates: 21-25 years)
- **Indian Military Academy (IMA)**: 19-24 years
- **Air Force Academy (AFA)**: 19-23 years
- **Naval Academy (NA)**: 19-22 years

### Educational Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree from a recognized university
- Must be a citizen of India
- Should be physically fit as per medical standards

## Exam Pattern

### Written Examination
The CDS written examination consists of:
1. **English** - 100 questions, 100 marks, 120 minutes
2. **General Knowledge** - 120 questions, 120 marks, 120 minutes
3. **Mathematics** - 100 questions, 100 marks, 120 minutes

### Important Points
- Multiple choice questions (MCQ format)
- 0.25 marks deducted for each wrong answer
- No sectional cutoff, only overall cutoff matters

## Selection Process

### Stage 1: Written Exam
This is the initial screening round where candidates must score above the cutoff to proceed.

### Stage 2: SSB Interview
Candidates qualifying the written exam appear for Service Selection Board (SSB) interview which lasts for 4-5 days.

### Stage 3: Medical Examination
Shortlisted candidates after SSB must clear the medical examination.

## Preparation Strategy

### Time Management
- Allocate 40% time to General Knowledge
- Allocate 35% time to Mathematics
- Allocate 25% time to English

### Study Resources
- Previous year question papers (last 5 years minimum)
- Standard reference books
- Mock tests and practice papers

## Key Tips for Success

1. **Consistency**: Regular study of 6-8 hours daily
2. **Current Affairs**: Stay updated with defense and national news
3. **Speed & Accuracy**: Practice speed with accuracy in mathematics
4. **Mock Tests**: Attempt at least 2-3 mock tests before the exam
5. **Revision**: Dedicate last 2 weeks for thorough revision

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Attempting all questions without strategy
- Negative marking - be selective in answering
- Ignoring current affairs questions in GK
- Not practicing previous year papers

## Conclusion

CDS is a achievable target with proper planning and dedicated preparation. Focus on conceptual clarity rather than cramming, and you'll definitely see success in your military career aspirations.

**Remember**: Quality of preparation is more important than quantity. Choose your study material wisely and stick to it!
